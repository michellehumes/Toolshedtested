## How to Choose an Angle Grinder

### 1) Corded vs Cordless

Corded grinders offer consistent power for long sessions. Cordless grinders offer flexibility and convenience but rely on battery runtime.

### 2) Size and Wheel Type

Most homeowners use 4.5-inch grinders. Larger grinders are more powerful but heavier and harder to control.

### 3) Safety Features

Look for a good guard design, a two-position handle, and a reliable power switch. Always use safety glasses and gloves.

### 4) Vibration Control

Lower vibration reduces fatigue. If you grind for long periods, this matters.

## FAQ

**Can I cut metal with an angle grinder?**
Yes. Use a thin cut-off wheel designed for metal.

**Do I need a high-powered grinder?**
Only if you do heavy grinding or frequent cutting. For light tasks, a standard 4.5-inch grinder is plenty.

**Is cordless strong enough?**
For many tasks, yes. Cordless grinders are great for quick cuts and jobsite use.

## Bottom Line

For most users, the DeWalt DWE402 is the best mix of price and performance. If you want cordless convenience, the Milwaukee M18 grinder is a strong pick. Choose based on the workload and how often you use it.
