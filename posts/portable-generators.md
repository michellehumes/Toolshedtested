## How to Choose a Portable Generator

### 1) Calculate Your Power Needs

Add up the running watts for the appliances you must power, and allow extra headroom for startup surges (fridges, pumps, and AC units can surge high).

### 2) Fuel Type and Runtime

Gas is common and easy to source. Dual-fuel models add flexibility. Pay attention to runtime at 50% load.

### 3) Noise and Location

Portable generators are loud. Use them outdoors, away from windows, and follow safety guidance for carbon monoxide.

### 4) Safety Features

Look for CO shutoff and overload protection. These are important for home use.

## FAQ

**Can a portable generator power my whole house?**
Typically no. Most portable generators are for essential circuits unless you use a transfer switch and manage loads carefully.

**Do I need a transfer switch?**
Yes if you want to connect it to home circuits safely. Otherwise, use extension cords rated for the load.

**How loud are portable generators?**
They are significantly louder than inverter generators. Plan for noise.

## Bottom Line

If you want the quietest portable power, the Honda EU3000iS is the premium choice. For most homeowners, the Westinghouse WGen7500 or Champion 7500 offer the best balance of price and power. Choose based on the loads you need to support.
