## How to Choose a Cordless Leaf Blower

### 1) Airflow vs Runtime

Higher power often means shorter runtime. For small yards, a lighter blower with longer runtime is more useful. For heavier leaf piles, power becomes more important.

### 2) Battery Platform

If you already own batteries, stick with that brand. Battery cost often exceeds the tool cost over time.

### 3) Weight and Comfort

A blower that feels too heavy becomes a chore quickly. If you have a larger yard, consider a backpack model or a balanced handheld.

### 4) Noise and Neighborhood Use

Cordless blowers are quieter than gas, which makes them better for early morning or evening use.

## FAQ

**Are cordless blowers powerful enough?**
Yes for most homeowner tasks, especially dry leaves and light debris. For heavy, wet leaf piles, a higher power unit helps.

**How many batteries do I need?**
If you have a medium or large yard, owning a second battery makes cleanup easier.

**Do cordless blowers replace gas blowers?**
For most homeowners, yes. Gas still has an edge for heavy commercial use.

## Bottom Line

For most people, EGO's 56V blower is the best all-around choice for power and convenience. If you want something lighter, DeWalt or Ryobi are excellent. Choose based on your yard size and battery system, and you will end up with a blower that is far easier to live with than gas.
